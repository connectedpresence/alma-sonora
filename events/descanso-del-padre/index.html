The invitation

An hour where nothing is asked of him

Fathers hold a lot. The job, the family, the quiet pressure of being the one others lean on. What they almost never get is an hour where nothing is asked of them.

This is that hour. No ceremony to learn, nothing to share, nothing to do. You lie down under the evening sky, and two gongs do the rest.

"I'm a father. I know what we carry — and I know what an hour of deep rest does for a nervous system that never gets to stand down. We built this evening for us."

Juan Saer · Co-facilitator

The evening

Arrival & settling

15 minutes

You're welcomed into the garden as the light goes low. Find your mat, lie down, arrive.

The sound journey

60 minutes

Two large gongs, crystal and Tibetan bowls. Deep tones that move through the body and tell the nervous system: you can stand down now.

Return

15 minutes

We come back slowly. A few minutes of quiet before the evening lets you go.
